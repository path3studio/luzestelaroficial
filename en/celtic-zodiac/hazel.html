The Hazel in the Celtic Zodiac

The Hazel, also known as Coll in the Ogham alphabet, is a tree sign in the Celtic zodiac, spanning the dates of August 5th through September 1st. This tree is deeply connected to the natural world, its roots symbolizing stability and its branches representing flexibility. In Celtic tradition, the Hazel is highly revered for its wisdom, protection, and fertility, reflecting the tree's ability to thrive in a wide range of environments and provide sustenance for countless creatures.

The Hazel's association with the Ogham letter Coll further underscores its connection to the natural world and the cycles of life. Coll is the third letter in the Ogham alphabet and is considered a symbol of wisdom, stability, and fertility. As a tree sign, the Hazel embodies these qualities, reminding us of the importance of living in harmony with the natural world and respecting the delicate balance of the ecosystem.

Hazel Personality

Individuals born under the sign of the Hazel are known for their wisdom, intelligence, and resourcefulness. They possess a deep understanding of the natural world and are often drawn to careers in fields such as science, philosophy, or environmental studies. Hazel people are also known for their ability to think creatively and find innovative solutions to complex problems, making them highly valued in their communities. However, their introspective nature can also make them appear reserved or aloof to others, and they may need to work on developing their communication skills to avoid misunderstandings.

One of the defining characteristics of the Hazel is their innate curiosity and love of learning. They are drawn to the mysteries of the universe and are often fascinated by the workings of the human mind and the natural world. This curiosity can sometimes lead them down tangents and make it difficult for them to focus on a single task, but it also drives them to continually seek out new knowledge and experiences. In terms of virtues, the Hazel is known for their integrity, loyalty, and sense of justice, making them highly respected and admired by others.

As with any tree sign, the Hazel has its shadows as well. They can be prone to anxiety and self-doubt, particularly when faced with uncertainty or change. This can sometimes lead them to become overly critical of themselves and others, but with self-awareness and practice, they can learn to cultivate a more positive and compassionate outlook. Ultimately, the Hazel's greatest strength lies in their ability to seek wisdom, cultivate their inner world, and live in harmony with the natural world.

Love and Relationships

In terms of romantic relationships, the Hazel is known for their deep emotional connection and profound understanding of the other person. They value loyalty, commitment, and honesty above all else and are willing to work through challenges to build a strong and lasting partnership. However, their introspective nature can sometimes make them appear distant or detached, and they may need to work on communicating their feelings and desires more openly with their partner. The Hazel is most compatible with the Oak and the Ash, as these trees share similar qualities and values.

When it comes to relationships, the Hazel is drawn to individuals who share their love of learning and their desire for personal growth. They value intellectual stimulation and enjoy engaging in deep conversations about philosophy, science, and the arts. In return, they offer a profound emotional connection and a deep sense of loyalty and commitment, making them a highly sought-after partner. However, their tendency to become overly absorbed in their own thoughts and feelings can sometimes make it difficult for them to connect with others, and they may need to work on developing their communication skills to avoid misunderstandings.
